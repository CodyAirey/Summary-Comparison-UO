Dinner has been laid out.<PARAGRAPH>Peggotty is sitting waiting and doing needlework.<PARAGRAPH>Mr. Peggotty comments that Peggotty has done right by Mr. Barkis, and Mr. Barkis has done right by her.<PARAGRAPH>The dreary Mrs. Gummidge groans at this.<PARAGRAPH>Mr. Peggotty tells Mrs. Gummidge to cheer up. If she cheered up a bit, she might find that she likes to be cheery.<PARAGRAPH>Mrs. Gummidge replies that being cheerful doesn't come naturally to her, since she is so lonesome.<PARAGRAPH>Mr. Peggotty places a lamp in the window to signal to little Emily that he's home and waiting for her.<PARAGRAPH>Peggotty thinks Mr. Peggotty's a bit babyish for doing this, but she seems fond of him for it. Mr. Peggotty wants to know his name.<PARAGRAPH>Ham looks at David, and suddenly David sits down from shock.<PARAGRAPH>Ham tells Mr. Peggotty that, for some time now, there has been a gentleman and a servant around Yarmouth.<PARAGRAPH>The night before, this servant was seen talking to little Emily.<PARAGRAPH>Ham begs David not to say the name, but David feels struck dumb, as though he can't move.<PARAGRAPH>Mr. Peggotty guesses it anyway: it's Steerforth.<PARAGRAPH>Ham tells David that he knows it's not David's fault, but that Steerforth is a \"damned villain\" (31.70).<PARAGRAPH>Mr. Peggotty takes his coat down from its peg on the wall.<PARAGRAPH>He's going to look for Emily, but first, he's going to sink Steerforth's boat.<PARAGRAPH>Mr. Peggotty is going to search for Emily throughout the world and bring her back to Yarmouth.<PARAGRAPH>Mrs. Gummidge starts up unexpectedly and tells him not to go out like that: she begs him to sit down and remember with her what it was like when he first adopted Ham and Emily and brought Mrs. Gummidge, the young widow, into his home.
